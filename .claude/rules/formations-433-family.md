# Formations: 4-3-3 Family (Four-at-the-Back)

Formations built around a three-man midfield spine with a back four.

---

## 4-3-3 ⭐ DEFAULT
**Line groups**: Defense | Mid (CDM, CM, CM) | Attack (RW, ST, LW)
**Positions**: GK · RB · CB · CB · LB · CDM · CM · CM · RW · ST · LW
**Strengths**: Midfield overload vs two-man systems; wide wingers pin back four defenders; balanced in all phases
**Weaknesses**: Lone striker can become isolated; wingers must track back; CDM must cover wide areas

## 4-1-4-1
**Line groups**: Defense | Def-Mid (CDM) | Mid (RM, CM, CM, LM) | Attack (ST)
**Positions**: GK · RB · CB · CB · LB · CDM · RM · CM · CM · LM · ST
**Strengths**: Compact and hard to break down; CDM shields back four; good transition base

## 4-3-2-1 (Christmas Tree)
**Line groups**: Defense | Mid (CM, CM, CM) | Att-Mid (CAM, CAM) | Attack (ST)
**Positions**: GK · RB · CB · CB · LB · CM · CM · CM · CAM · CAM · ST
**Strengths**: Five midfielders control central pitch; two CAMs create around lone striker; compact defensively
**Weaknesses**: Extremely narrow without wingers; demands world-class players; lone striker handles multiple roles

## 4-5-1
**Line groups**: Defense | Mid (RM, CM, CM, CM, LM) | Attack (ST)
**Positions**: GK · RB · CB · CB · LB · RM · CM · CM · CM · LM · ST
**Strengths**: Width across entire midfield; midfielders create central overloads; good defensive shape
**Weaknesses**: Lone striker isolated and heavily relied upon; wide mids caught between roles; demands excellent passing and stamina

## 4-6-0 (Striker-less)
**Line groups**: Defense | Def-Mid (CDM, CDM) | Mid (CM, CM) | Att-Mid (CAM, CAM)
**Positions**: GK · RB · CB · CB · LB · CDM · CDM · CM · CM · CAM · CAM
**Strengths**: Dominates midfield with six players; four attacking mids create from unmarked positions; unique defensive problems for opponents
**Weaknesses**: No recognised goalscorer; demands exceptional intelligence and pace from front four; rarely used consistently
