# Formations: 3-5-2 Family (Wing-Back / Three-at-the-Back)

Formations using three centre-backs with wing-backs and a five-man midfield spine.

---

## 3-5-2
**Line groups**: Defense (CB, CB, CB) | Mid (LWB, CM, CM, CM, RWB) | Attack (ST, ST)
**Positions**: GK · CB · CB · CB · LWB · CM · CM · CM · RWB · ST · ST
**Strengths**: Five midfielders dominate possession; three CBs outnumber two strikers; wing-backs offer dual attacking-defensive threat
**Weaknesses**: Wing-backs must cover entire flank; gaps left when they advance; wide CBs drawn out can be exploited

## 3-5-1-1
**Line groups**: Defense (CB, CB, CB) | Mid (LWB, CM, CM, CM, RWB) | Att-Mid (CAM) | Attack (ST)
**Positions**: GK · CB · CB · CB · LWB · CM · CM · CM · RWB · CAM · ST
**Strengths**: Midfield overload enables pitch control; CAM links mids to ST with creative freedom; defensive flexibility with numbers
**Weaknesses**: Wide flanks vulnerable without traditional full-backs; isolated striker relies heavily on CAM support; complex execution

## 5-3-2
**Line groups**: Defense (LWB, CB, CB, CB, RWB) | Mid (CM, CM, CM) | Attack (ST, ST)
**Positions**: GK · LWB · CB · CB · CB · RWB · CM · CM · CM · ST · ST
**Strengths**: Extremely solid defensive foundation; five defenders plus three mids deny space; two strikers provide dangerous counter-attacking outlet
**Weaknesses**: Limited attacking options; midfield trio leaves wide gaps; requires very hard-working midfielders
